Services


Preventative Care


Medford Animal Hospital believes the best way to keep your pet healthy is through preventative medicine. Preventative medicine begins with yearly physical examinations where we will encourage you to inform us of any changes in your pet's weight, appetite, thirst, energy level, or any other sign that seems out of the ordinary. We will listen to your pet's heart and lungs, examine teeth for dental disease, check for joint discomfort such as arthritis, and look for any swelling or lumps, among other procedures.

During your annual visit we will discuss our recommendations for vaccinations, nutrition, flea and tick prevention, as well as screening for heartworm and any other parasitic diseases.

Annual exams are important in order to detect problems before they become more difficult and more costly to treat.

Vaccinations


Vaccinations are an important part of your pet's healthcare. Veterinary medicine has made wonderful advances in immunizations, and your pet can now be protected against most major infections diseases.

Vaccines can protect against diseases such as feline leukemia, panleukopenia, respiratory diseases, as well as rabies. Vaccines are also useful in preventing canine distemper, parvovorius, bordetella (kennel cough), and Lyme disease.

At Medford Animal Hospital, we do not believe in over-vaccination and tailor our protocol to fit each animal's lifestyle. We are happy to discuss any questions or concerns you may have regarding your pet's specific vaccination protocol.

Dentistry


We recommend routine oral exams as well as prophylactic dental cleanings. If left untreated, tooth and gum disease can cause chronic pain and lead to bacteria entering the bloodstream, resulting in systemic illness and damage to the heart, kidneys, and liver.

Parasite Control


We recommend bringing a stool sample to your pet's exam for us to perform a fecal test for common intestinal parasites at our hospital. Some common intestinal parasites include tapeworms, hookworms, roundworms, whipworm, giardia, and coccidia.

If it is found that your pet has parasites, we will provide a medication from our pharmacy for treatment.
